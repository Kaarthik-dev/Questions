Key activities:

Log correlation across multiple sources

Timeline reconstruction

User, host, and network behavior analysis

Severity and impact assessment

Containment is the phase where the SOC limits the spread and impact of a confirmed incident.
The goal is to stop further damage while preserving evidence and minimizing business disruption.

Common containment actions:

Isolating affected hosts

Disabling compromised accounts

Blocking malicious IPs/domains

Restricting network access

Eradication focuses on eliminating the root cause of the incident.

Removing malware and persistence mechanisms

Closing exploited vulnerabilities

Resetting credentials and secrets

Applying security patches and configuration fixes

Recovery is the phase where affected systems, services, and data are safely restored to normal operation.

Key steps:

System and data restoration from clean backups

Validation and integrity checks

Post-recovery monitoring for reinfection

Gradual reintroduction to production
